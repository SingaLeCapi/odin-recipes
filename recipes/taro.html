Bamileke Taro

Description

Ingredients:

  • 3 lbs Meats/fish
  • 1 (scotch bonnet) pepper - optional, hot
  • 4 lbs Cocoyams, small Canned Goods
  • 4 cups Beef stock Baking & Spices
  • 2 tsp Achu spices, powdered
  • 2 tsp Baking soda
  • 2g Salt
  • 2 Seasoning
  • 1/2 cup Palm oil

Steps

  1. Warm palm oil in a skillet (frying pan) for about 1 minute. Do NOT bleach it. Pour the all ingredients except the meat and fish into a blender. Pulse until it is well mixed and yellowish. Taste to ensure that seasoning is perfect. Add a little salt if needed.You might find some bubbles at the top of the soup. That is totally okay.
  2. Pour over the meat/fish and mix. Alternately, you could keep the meat seperately from the soup and put it on the side while serving. Serve with some achu!
  3. Wash cocoyams and place in a pot with skin on. If also using large cocoyams (“mami coco”) and “achu banana”, place the large cocoyams at the bottom of the pot then add in the small cocoyams.
  4. Boil until cocoyams are soft and the skin comes off easily.
  5. While they are still warm, peel and process to a paste in a food processor. Traditionally, they are pounded in a mortar but a food processor can do the job though it might not give you a very smooth paste.
