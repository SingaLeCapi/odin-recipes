Bamileke Koki

Description

Koki Beans is a traditional dish of my village in Cameroon. It is also one of the favourite dishes of many Cameroonians back home, as well as the diaspora. The Koki Beans looks like a cake, yellow in colour, soft and very tasty.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup palm oil
  • 1/2 Scotch bonnet or habanero pepper optional
  • 3 teaspoon salt or more
  • 3 cups chopped fresh spinach
  • 3 1/2 cups water

Steps

  1. Soak the dry beans in a good quantity of water to soak overnight.
  2. The next day, wash the beans by rubbing them between your hands and changing water. When the beans are clean, sieve and put them aside.
  3. Blend the beans and habanero pepper using a blender. Avoid putting a lot of water while blending. Pour the blend into a large bowl.
  4. Warm the palm oil into a pan, just bring it to a lukewarm state.
  5. Mix the blend for a while, then add salt and the lukewarm palm oil progressively. Keep mixing until it is well combined.
  6. Pour lukewarm water into a bowl, add the limestone (if you have it) into it and mix.
  7. Add that water into the koki mixture and mix. Have a taste to check if the salt is enough, if not adjust.
  8. Clean the Banana leaves. Take 2 leaves at a time and place them crosswise (on top of each other) over an eating bowl
  9. Pour in a small portion of the koki mixture. Join the ends of the leaves to the centre and tie it with a kitchen string
  10. Pour a cup of water into a big pot, place a leaf at the bottom, then place the bundle into the pot. Let it cook for at least 1 hour. Make sure you check and add water regularly to avoid burns.
